Terms of Use – Cytometry & Microscopy Core Facility

1) Scientific publications (acknowledgment, information, and co-authorship)

  • Any scientific publication arising from data acquired on the core facility or from support provided by the facility must include an acknowledgment.
  • The facility staff must be kept informed of publications related to work performed at the facility (ideally before submission to validate the acknowledgment and, if needed, upon acceptance for our records).
  • If specific grants/funding numbers support the instruments, they must be mentioned (the facility can provide the proper codes).
  • If support goes beyond routine (experimental design, acquisition/expert settings, analysis/interpretation, writing), co-authorship should be discussed and agreed upon with the facility team before submission.
“We acknowledge Author Author (YourUniversity) for training and technical/scientific assistance, and the YourFacility core facility for access to the equipment.”

2) Proper use and care of equipment

  • Mandatory training before any use; access may be restricted based on competency level.
  • Use only approved consumables validated by the facility; do not use incompatible products.
  • Cleaning and restoring the setup at the end of the session (best practices covered during training).
  • Immediate reporting of any incident, anomaly, breakage, or maintenance alert via “Report a problem”.

Forbidden:

  • Eating or drinking near instruments.
  • Changing protected settings or bypassing access controls.
  • Installing/using unauthorized software on acquisition stations.
  • Storing data long-term on acquisition PCs (export them at the end of your session).

Repair costs due to proven negligence may be charged to the responsible research unit.

3) Bookings, delays, and assistance

  • Modify/Cancel: allowed up to 30 minutes after the start of the booking. Past this delay, the booking is fully charged.
  • Assistance: provided subject to staff availability. Please plan ahead.

4) Data, security, and confidentiality

  • Export your data at the end of the session to your institutional storage.
  • Comply with YourUniversity policies (security, confidentiality, ethics).
  • Institutional email address: You must provide an institutional or professional e‑mail address (e.g., admin@youruniversity.ca). It will be used only for reservation‑related notifications.
  • Contactability: By accepting the regulation, you consent to being contacted at this address by platform staff and other users.

5) Graduated sanctions

  1. Written reminder.
  2. Warning and/or invoicing (no-show, damage, lack of cleaning).
  3. Temporary suspension of access.
  4. Access removal and notification to the lab management.

6) Contacts

  • Support / incident: “Report a problem” button (home page).
  • Manager: Author Author – admin@youruniversity.ca
  • YourFacility: Official website
